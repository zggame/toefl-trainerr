# TOEFL Speaking Research — Task 6: Sample Responses & Benchmark Library

**Last updated:** 2026-04-20

---

## 1. Overview

High-scoring sample responses and examiner commentary serve multiple purposes in the app:
- **Calibration** — learners hear what a score-4 response actually sounds like
- **Score explanation** — annotate why a sample scored well at each dimension
- **Comparison** — learners compare their answer to a strong benchmark
- **Few-shot evaluation prompts** — anchor AI scoring with examples
- **Learning material** — teach structure, vocabulary, and delivery patterns

---

## 2. Sample Response Library Structure

### 2.1 Score-Level Samples

For each task type, collect 1–2 audio samples at each score level:

| Score | Purpose | What to include |
|-------|---------|----------------|
| 5–6 | Excellent benchmark | Annotated transcript + commentary + audio |
| 4–5 | Good benchmark | Annotated transcript + commentary + audio |
| 3 | Average/Limit benchmark | Annotated transcript + commentary + audio |
| 1–2 | What to avoid | Annotated transcript + commentary + audio |

### 2.2 Task-Type Coverage

| Task Type | Number of sample sets needed |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| Independent (Task 1 style) | 4 samples (one per score level) |
| Integrated Campus (Task 2 style) | 4 samples |
| Integrated Academic (Task 3/4 style) | 4 samples |
| New Interview (2023+ format) | 4 samples |

**Total:** ~16 audio samples minimum for a complete benchmark library.

### 2.3 Sample Response Format

Each sample should include:

1. **Audio file** — MP3 or M4A, 45s–2min depending on task
2. **Transcript** — word-for-word transcript with timestamps
3. **Scorer commentary** — human-rater style explanation of why it scored at that level
4. **Dimension breakdown** — Delivery / Language Use / Topic Development scores
5. **Annotation layer** — markers highlighting specific strengths and weaknesses in the transcript

### Example Annotation Format:

```
[Transcript]
Speaker: Well, the most important experience I had was... um... working 
at a local restaurant. I think it taught me a lot about [0:15] 
communication and teamwork. [0:20]

[Annotation]
[0:15] Good use of academic vocabulary (communication, teamwork)
[0:20] Transition marker used effectively (I think)
[um] First filler — minor, acceptable at this score level
[~80 words in 30 seconds] — appropriate length for 45s task
```

## 3. Examiner Commentary Style Guide

The commentary should follow ETS rater reasoning style:

### For Delivery:
- Describe the overall clarity and fluency
- Note specific pronunciation patterns
- Mention pacing and naturalness
- Flag fillers, hesitations, or unnatural pauses

### For Language Use:
- Describe grammatical accuracy
- Note vocabulary range and appropriateness
- Mention error patterns (without listing every error)
- Comment on sentence complexity

### For Topic Development:
- Describe whether the response fully addresses the prompt
- Note the quality of supporting details
- Comment on organization and coherence
- Mention response length adequacy

### Example commentary:

**Score 4 response to: Describe a place you enjoy visiting.**

> This response demonstrates strong delivery with clear pronunciation and natural pacing throughout. The speaker maintained a consistent flow with only minor hesitation markers (two brief fillers). Grammar is generally accurate with appropriate complex sentence structures. Vocabulary is well-suited to the topic with relevant word choices (serene, retreat, escape). The response is well-developed with two specific supporting details (weekly visits, reading habit) and a clear concluding statement. Organization is logical, moving from the main claim to supporting examples to a brief reflection. This response fully meets the expectations for a score of 4.

## 4. Sources for Sample Collection

### Official sources:
- ETS TOEFL Practice test (free sample available on ETS website)
- TOEFL Practice Online (TPO) — official paid practice tests
- ETS TOEFL Speaking rubrics PDF

### Reputable secondary sources:
- Magoosh TOEFL practice tests
- E2 Language YouTube (high-quality spoken examples)
- TOEFL Master YouTube
- GregMat (high-quality TOEFL prep, especially speaking templates)
- IELTS Liz (cross-applicable speaking examples)

### Note on copyrighted audio:
ETS owns copyright to official TOEFL recordings. The app should create original sample responses for the benchmark library (with consent), or use third-party sources that have licensed the material.

**Alternative:** Use AI-generated sample responses calibrated to score-level descriptors as the benchmark library. This is legally cleaner and can be curated for quality.

## 5. AI Few-Shot Evaluation Prompts

Sample responses can anchor AI scoring prompts for higher consistency:

```
You are evaluating a TOEFL speaking response. Use the following 
calibration examples to score consistently.

EXAMPLE 1 (Score 4):
[transcript here]
Why this scores 4: Clear delivery, accurate grammar, relevant 
details, well-organized. Full response addresses the prompt with 
specific supporting examples.

EXAMPLE 2 (Score 2):
[transcript here]
Why this scores 2: Frequent hesitation, limited vocabulary, 
response too short, vague content without specific details.

Now evaluate this response:
[transcript here]
```
